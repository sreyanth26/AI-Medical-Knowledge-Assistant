SECTION 1: HYPERTENSION (HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE)
========================================

DEFINITION
Hypertension is defined as persistently elevated blood pressure:
- Stage 1: Systolic 130–139 mmHg OR Diastolic 80–89 mmHg
- Stage 2: Systolic ≥140 mmHg OR Diastolic ≥90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: Systolic >180 and/or Diastolic >120 mmHg

CAUSES
Primary (Essential) Hypertension — 90–95% of cases:
- No identifiable single cause
- Risk factors: age, obesity, sedentary lifestyle, high salt intake, alcohol, family history, stress

Secondary Hypertension — 5–10% of cases:
- Renal: Chronic kidney disease, renal artery stenosis
- Endocrine: Primary aldosteronism, Cushing's syndrome, pheochromocytoma, thyroid disease
- Obstructive sleep apnea
- Medications: NSAIDs, oral contraceptives, corticosteroids, decongestants

SYMPTOMS
Often called "silent killer" — most patients have NO symptoms.
Severe/emergency signs: Severe headache (occipital), visual disturbances, chest pain,
dyspnea, nausea, neurological symptoms (hypertensive emergency)

DIAGNOSIS
- Office BP: Average of 2 readings, 2 occasions, 5 minutes apart
- Ambulatory BP Monitoring (ABPM): 24-hour monitoring (gold standard)
- Home BP Monitoring: Useful for white-coat hypertension

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS (First-line for Stage 1)
- DASH diet: Low sodium (<2.3g/day), high potassium, fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy
- Weight loss: Each 1kg reduction lowers BP by ~1 mmHg
- Physical activity: 150 minutes moderate exercise/week
- Limit alcohol: Men <2 drinks/day; Women <1 drink/day
- Quit smoking
- Stress management

PHARMACOLOGICAL TREATMENT

First-line Drug Classes (A-C-D rule):
A — ACE Inhibitors / ARBs: Lisinopril, Ramipril, Amlodipine, Losartan, Valsartan
C — Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs): Amlodipine 5–10mg OD (preferred in Black patients, elderly)
D — Diuretics (Thiazide-type): Hydrochlorothiazide, Chlorthalidone, Indapamide

Beta-blockers: Not first-line unless angina, heart failure, or post-MI

Combination Therapy:
- Most patients require 2+ drugs for BP control
- Preferred combo: ACE inhibitor/ARB + CCB or Thiazide
- Avoid: ACE inhibitor + ARB combination (increases renal risk)

TARGET BP
- General: <130/80 mmHg
- Elderly (>65 years): <130/80 mmHg if tolerated
- CKD with proteinuria: <130/80 mmHg
- Diabetes: <130/80 mmHg

MONITORING
- BP check: Every 1–3 months until controlled, then every 6–12 months
- Annual: Renal function, electrolytes, fasting glucose, lipid profile, ECG
- Screen for end-organ damage: Eyes (retinopathy), kidneys (proteinuria), heart (LVH)

SECTION 2: TYPE 2 DIABETES MELLITUS
========================================

DEFINITION
Type 2 Diabetes: Chronic metabolic disorder characterized by:
- Insulin resistance (cells don't respond to insulin)
- Progressive beta-cell dysfunction (insufficient insulin production)
- Resulting in hyperglycemia (high blood glucose)

DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA (Any one of the following):
- Fasting plasma glucose ≥126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L) on 2 occasions
- 2-hour glucose ≥200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) during OGTT
- HbA1c ≥6.5% (confirmed on repeat testing)
- Random glucose ≥200 mg/dL with classic symptoms

Prediabetes:
- Fasting: 100–125 mg/dL (Impaired Fasting Glucose)
- HbA1c: 5.7–6.4%

RISK FACTORS
- Obesity (BMI ≥25, especially central obesity)
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Family history (first-degree relative with T2DM)
- Gestational diabetes history
- Hypertension, dyslipidemia
- PCOS
- Age ≥45 years
- Ethnicity: South Asian, African, Hispanic (higher risk)

SYMPTOMS
Classic "3 Ps":
- Polyuria (excessive urination)
- Polydipsia (excessive thirst)
- Polyphagia (excessive hunger)
Other: Weight loss, fatigue, blurred vision, slow wound healing, frequent infections

TREATMENT ALGORITHM

Step 1 — Lifestyle + Metformin
- Metformin 500mg OD/BD, titrate to 2000mg/day
- Target HbA1c: <7% (individualise — <8% for elderly/frail)

Step 2 — Add second agent if HbA1c not at target after 3 months:
Choose based on patient profile:
- With cardiovascular disease: Add SGLT2 inhibitor (Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin) or GLP-1 agonist (Liraglutide, Semaglutide)
- With heart failure or CKD: Prefer SGLT2 inhibitor
- Need weight loss: GLP-1 agonist
- Cost concern: Sulfonylurea (Glipizide, Glimepiride) or Pioglitazone

Step 3 — Add third agent or insulin if still not at target

Insulin Initiation:
- Basal insulin (Glargine or NPH): 10 units at bedtime OR 0.1–0.2 units/kg
- Titrate by 1–2 units every 3 days until fasting glucose 80–130 mg/dL

MONITORING
- HbA1c: Every 3 months until stable, then every 6 months
- Self-monitoring blood glucose (SMBG): Fasting and 2h post-meal
- Annual screening:
  * Urine albumin-creatinine ratio (kidney)
  * Fundoscopy (diabetic retinopathy)
  * Foot examination (peripheral neuropathy, peripheral artery disease)
  * Lipid profile
  * Blood pressure

HYPOGLYCEMIA MANAGEMENT
Blood glucose <70 mg/dL (3.9 mmol/L):
Mild/Moderate: 15g fast-acting carbohydrates (glucose tablets, 150mL juice, 3 sugar sachets)
Reassess after 15 minutes — repeat if still <70 mg/dL ("15-15 rule")
Severe (unconscious): 1mg glucagon IM or IV Dextrose 50%

COMPLICATIONS
Microvascular: Diabetic nephropathy, retinopathy, neuropathy
Macrovascular: Coronary artery disease, stroke, peripheral artery disease
Other: Diabetic foot, recurrent infections, erectile dysfunction

SECTION 3: ASTHMA
========================================

DEFINITION
Asthma is a chronic inflammatory airway disease characterized by:
- Variable airflow obstruction (usually reversible)
- Airway hyperresponsiveness
- Chronic airway inflammation

SYMPTOMS
- Episodic wheezing (musical whistling sound on expiration)
- Chest tightness
- Shortness of breath (dyspnea)
- Cough (often worse at night and early morning)
Triggers: Allergens, exercise, cold air, infections, smoke, NSAIDs

DIAGNOSIS
- Spirometry: FEV1/FVC ratio <0.7 with ≥12% and 200mL improvement after bronchodilator
- Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF): Variability >10% confirms diagnosis
- FeNO (Fractional exhaled Nitric Oxide): Elevated in eosinophilic asthma

SEVERITY AND STEP THERAPY

Step 1 (Mild intermittent):
- SABA (Salbutamol/Albuterol 100mcg MDI) PRN only — no daily controller

Step 2 (Mild persistent):
- Low-dose Inhaled Corticosteroid (ICS): Budesonide 100–200mcg BD OR Beclometasone 100–200mcg BD
- Or: LTRA (Montelukast 10mg OD) if ICS not tolerated

Step 3 (Moderate persistent):
- Low-dose ICS + LABA: Budesonide/Formoterol 160/4.5mcg BD OR Fluticasone/Salmeterol

Step 4 (Severe):
- Medium-high dose ICS + LABA
- Add LAMA (Tiotropium) or LTRA
- Consider biologic therapy (Omalizumab for allergic asthma, Mepolizumab for eosinophilic)

ACUTE SEVERE ASTHMA — MANAGEMENT
- Sit patient upright, high-flow oxygen (target SpO2 94–98%)
- Salbutamol nebulization: 2.5–5mg every 20 minutes for 1 hour
- Ipratropium bromide: 0.5mg nebulized every 20 minutes x 3 doses
- IV Hydrocortisone 200mg OR Oral Prednisolone 40–50mg
- IV Magnesium Sulfate 1.2–2g over 20 minutes (severe cases)
- If no improvement: ICU, IV Aminophylline, or mechanical ventilation
